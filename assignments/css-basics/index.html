A Little About Jim

James Robert "Jim" Jarmusch was born in 1953 in Cuyahoga Falls, Ohio. His mother was a film and theater reviewer, and introduced Jarmusch to films when she would leave him at the theater to watch matinee double features while she ran errands.
After graduating high school he moved to Chicago, going to journalism school, but upon a lack of completing any work, he left. Jarmusch went on to study literature at Columbia University.

Jim's Continued Path

Jarmusch then lived in New York City for a while, broke and working as a musician. This is when he followed up on his youth film interest--he went to film graduate school.
Jarmusch went on to create classic films, such as his first major film "Stranger Than Paradise," "Down by Law," and "Night on Earth."

My Favorite Jarmusch Film

One of my personal favorites of his work is the 2003 film "Coffee and Cigarettes." This work is a collection of many different short films, each one a wildly unpredictable match of (fairly famous) actors and/or musicians. The pairs sit and enjoy coffee and cigarettes together, sharing conversation.
You see Bill Murray be the waiter to the Wu-Tang Clan, Iggy Pop and Tom Waits annoy each other, you even see Cate Blanchett hangout with herself. Check it out!
